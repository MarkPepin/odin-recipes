Beef Bourguignon

Winter's Ultimate Comfort Food

The frost has entered and snow is on the horizon. I don't what it is about Fall/Winter but certain foods just taste better. Don't get me wrong beef bourguignon is always great but something about eating it on a chilly day after some hard work outside just send it through the stratosphere!

Now sure we could probably crank this dish out in a few hours but let's do it right and take our time with this one. Trust me it is worth it!

Ingredients for Beef Marinade

  • 2 lbs of Chuck Roast
  • 2 lg Carrots
  • 20 Pearl Onions
  • Bay Leaf
  • 3 Sprigs Thyme
  • 1 Bottle of Pinot Noir/Burgundy

Ingredients for Browning

  • 3 TBSP Oil
  • 3/4 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Pepper

Ingredients for Stew

  • 7 oz Mushrooms (halved or quartered)
  • 5 oz Bacon (chopped into pieces)
  • 3 TBSP Butter
  • 3 Cloves Garlic (minced)
  • 2 TBSP Tomato Paste
  • 6 TBSP Flour
  • 3 Cups Beef Stock
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Marinate Beef in marinade for 24 hours
  2. Strain and reserve red wine & preheat oven to 350
  3. Pour into saucepan & reduce in half, skim scum (about 7 min)
  4. Pat beef dry & season with salt and pepper
  5. Brown beef in dutch oven, once browned remove into bowl
  6. Cook bacon next and remove
  7. Cook onions, until you brown patches, remove to separate bowl
  8. Cook mushrooms put into same bowl as onions
  9. add butter, melt, add carrots, cook 3-4min, add garlic cook for 1 minute
  10. Add tomato paste, cook for 2 min
  11. Add flour cook for 2 min
  12. Add stock slowly while stirring to eliminate clumps
  13. Add wine and mix
  14. Add to pot beef, bacon, thyme, salt, and pepper
  15. Bring to boil reduce to simmer cover and put in oven for 1 hour
  16. Remove and stir in mushrooms and onions
  17. Return to oven for 1.5 hours or until beef falls apart
